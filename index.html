Runner's Tip!

Cambridge can get busy on weekends, especially along the river! Try an early morning or weekday run for a more peaceful experience and to fully enjoy the scenery.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these routes suitable for cycling too?

Some routes, especially those along the river or through parks like Milton Country Park, are great for cycling. Always be mindful of pedestrians and check local signage for any cycling restrictions, particularly in very busy areas or specific college grounds.

How do I find these spots and routes?

We provide general descriptions and names. We recommend using a map app (like Google Maps, Komoot, or OS Maps) with the spot name or a nearby landmark for precise starting points and navigation. For country parks, search for their official websites for visitor information.

Classic River Cam Path

Distance: 7km (Out and back)

Difficulty: Easy

Enjoy a flat, scenic run or walk along the iconic River Cam, passing boathouses and green spaces. Start near the city centre.

More Details:

This iconic route is perfect for all abilities. Starting near Scudamore's Mill Lane Punting Station, follow the well-paved path northwards. You'll pass St John's College, the 'Backs' of several colleges, and numerous boathouses. Continue past Midsummer Common and Stourbridge Common.

The path can get busy, especially on sunny weekends. Consider an early morning or weekday run for a quieter experience. Excellent for flat, consistent pacing. You can turn back at any point to adjust the distance.

Grantchester Meadows Loop

Distance: 5km

Difficulty: Easy

A beautiful loop through the famous meadows where cows graze freely. Perfect for a relaxed pace and enjoying nature.

More Details:

Escape the city bustle with this idyllic loop. Start from the Lammas Land car park or by walking down Grantchester Street. The path through the meadows is mostly grass, so it can be muddy after rain. Enjoy views of King's College Chapel in the distance. You might share the path with grazing cows!

You can extend the route by continuing towards Grantchester village for a refreshment stop at The Orchard Tea Garden before looping back. A very family-friendly walk.

Milton Country Park Trails

Distance: 3-6km (Various loops)

Difficulty: Easy to Moderate

Explore varied trails around lakes and through woodlands. Great for trail running or a family walk. Pay & display parking.

More Details:

Located just north of Cambridge, Milton Country Park offers a network of well-maintained gravel and dirt paths winding around several lakes and through diverse woodlands. There are marked trails of varying lengths, making it easy to customize your run or walk from 3km to 6km or more.

It's a popular spot for dog walkers and families. Look out for varied birdlife. Paid parking is available. Check their website for opening times and any events.

Wandlebury & Gog Magog Hills

Distance: 8-12km

Difficulty: Moderate

Slightly further afield, offering rolling terrain with gentle hills and expansive views. Ideal for a longer hike or a more challenging run.

More Details:

For those seeking a bit more elevation and a taste of chalk grassland, Wandlebury Country Park (a former Iron Age hillfort) and the adjacent Gog Magog Hills offer fantastic views. Trails are a mix of woodland paths and open fields.

The 'Gogs' provide some of the only real hills in the immediate Cambridge area, great for hill repeats or a more strenuous hike. Managed by Cambridge Past, Present & Future. Parking available (charges may apply).

Fen Rivers Way (Cambridge to Waterbeach)

Distance: ~10km (One way)

Difficulty: Moderate

Follow a section of this long-distance path along the River Cam. Mostly flat but can be muddy. Train back from Waterbeach for a one-way trip.

More Details:

This section of the long-distance Fen Rivers Way offers a true Fenland experience. The path follows the River Cam (or sometimes a parallel lode) northwards from Cambridge. It's very flat but can be exposed to wind.

The surface is mixed, sometimes grassy, sometimes a bit rougher, and prone to muddiness after wet weather. It's great for seeing Fenland wildlife. The ~10km stretch to Waterbeach is popular, with a convenient train station in Waterbeach for the return journey to Cambridge.

Cambridge "Green Ring" Challenge

Distance: 20km+

Difficulty: Hard

A challenging, longer route circumnavigating Cambridge, linking various green spaces, towpaths, and quieter roads. Requires good stamina and navigation.

More Details:

This is a more ambitious, self-devised challenge for experienced runners or long-distance walkers. The concept is to link up various green spaces, cycle paths, towpaths, and quieter residential streets to create a loop of 20km or more circumnavigating the city.

It requires good navigation skills (using a map app like Komoot or Strava route builder is recommended) and self-sufficiency. Expect varied terrain. A great way to explore the different facets of Cambridge's periphery.
